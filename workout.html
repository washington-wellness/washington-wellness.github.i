Workouts

Every workout follows a specific program design. Workout programs vary based on the client's goals. The following workout regime are offered below:

Rehabilitation Exercise

Rehab and correctional exercise templates are designed to the client or patient needs. This workout regime is based on slow isolated controlled movements to get muscles to "fire" efficiently.

Strength Training

Muscle hypertrophy is the scientific evidence of muscle growth. This occurs as a result of strength or resistance training that stimulates cell activity in muscle fibers activating them to grow. Hypertrophy will not occur from an aerobic based exercise regimen.

Strength and Conditioning

Strength and Conditioning programs are tailored to the athlete/client needs. This exercise regimen involves the combination of strength exercise and conditioning work to help advance an athletes physical performance.

Core training

The anterior and posterior core are the main components of the human body for daily function, mobility and balance. Core workout programs involve multiple movement patterns to improve "core stability"

ART (Active Release Techniques)

ART is a form of myofascia release (therapy) involving the release of restricted muscle tissue fiber to help the human body mechanics function properly.

HIIT Training (high intensity interval training)

HIIT training is a workout program designed around aerobic based exercise training exercise targeted around weight loss. When the heart rate is elevated you go into a "fat burning zone" This program is focused on weight loss and training the heart rate to have a slower resting heart rate. The lower your heart rate the better your HIIT training exercise program will become.
